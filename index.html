PhysOmni: Physics-Grounded Multi-Object Scene Generation from a Single Image in Real Time

Anonymous
Paper arXiv Code

PhysOmni facilitates holistic 3D scene generation and physically grounded video synthesis from a single input image.

Abstract

Recent generative video models achieve high visual quality but remain fundamentally limited in physical consistency and controllability. Video generation methods are often physically uncontrollable, while single-image to 3D approaches frequently suffer from multi-object inter-penetration.

We propose PhysOmni, a training-free framework that transforms a single image into a physically consistent and controllable video through holistic scene-level 3D reconstruction. By modeling the entire scene geometry within a unified spatial representation, PhysOmni resolves inter-object penetration and alignment ambiguities, enabling precise multi-object interaction and mechanics-based control such as force application and rigid-body dynamics.

Unlike diffusion-based or autoregressive video priors with high inference latency, our approach supports instantaneous physics simulation and real-time rendering once initialized. Experiments demonstrate that PhysOmni significantly outperforms existing methods in physical fidelity, spatial coherence, and controllability.

Issues

Prevalent issues in existing methods. Video generation suffers from physically uncontrollable motion; single-image-to-3D reconstruction exhibits multi-object inter-penetration; physics-based scene-level 3D generation shows spatial misalignment and inconsistency with the input image; and physics-based 3D generation and control often results in cartoonish appearance.

Pipeline

Overview of the PhysOmni framework. A single input image is converted into a physically consistent dynamic scene via four key stages:

  • (1) Scene Perception: Decomposes the input into geometric primitives and a holistic background.
  • (2) Scene Alignment: Maps all entities into a unified world coordinate frame to ensure spatial consistency.
  • (3) Physics Simulation: Computes complex multibody dynamics and contact constraints.
  • (4) Video-Generation-based Rendering: Synthesizes high-fidelity, temporally coherent video from the simulated physical trajectories.
